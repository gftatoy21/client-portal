💉 Tirzepatide Self-Injection Guide

How to Safely Inject
Your Tirzepatide

This guide was prepared by your physician at MedGlow Wellness. Please read it fully before your first injection. When in doubt — always message us first. 🌿

⚠️ Before You Begin — Read This

  • Never inject if you feel unwell, dizzy, or have low blood sugar
  • Always wash your hands thoroughly before handling any supplies
  • Never share your vial, syringes, or needles with anyone
  • Do not inject into skin that is irritated, bruised, or scarred
  • If this is your first injection — message MedGlow Wellness first ✉️
1
Gather Your Supplies
📦
▼

Everything you need is inside your MedGlow Wellness box:

  • 1 vial of Tirzepatide 15mg (lyophilized powder)
  • 1 vial of BAC Water 3ml (bacteriostatic water for reconstitution)
  • 1 x 3cc syringe (for reconstitution)
  • Insulin syringes 1ml (for injection)
  • Alcohol swabs
  • Gloves
💡 Store your reconstituted vial in the refrigerator (2–8°C). Never freeze. Keep away from direct light.
2
Reconstitute the Vial
🧪
▼

Tirzepatide comes as a powder and needs to be mixed with BAC Water before use. Do this only once — the mixed vial will last the full program.

  • Wipe the tops of both vials with a fresh alcohol swab — let dry 30 seconds
  • Aspirate 1.5ml of BAC Water using the 3cc syringe
  • Slowly inject the BAC Water into the Tirzepatide vial — aim at the glass wall, not directly onto the powder
  • Gently swirl the vial in a circular motion until the powder is fully dissolved — do NOT shake
  • The solution should be clear and colorless — discard if cloudy or has particles
💡 Adding BAC Water slowly and swirling (not shaking) prevents the peptide from denaturing (breaking down). This step protects the potency of your medication.
3

4
Choose Your Injection Site
📍
▼

Tirzepatide is a subcutaneous injection — meaning it goes into the fatty tissue just under the skin. Rotate sites every week:

🫃
Abdomen
2 inches away from your belly button. Most common site.
🦵
Thigh
Outer front area of either thigh. Easy to self-inject.
💪
Upper Arm
Back of upper arm. May need assistance for self-injection.
💡 Rotate sites every week — e.g. right abdomen → left abdomen → right thigh → left thigh. This prevents skin hardening (lipohypertrophy) at one spot.
5
Prepare the Injection Area
🧼
▼
  • Wash hands thoroughly with soap and water for at least 20 seconds
  • Put on your gloves
  • Clean the chosen injection site with an alcohol swab using a circular motion
  • Wait 30 seconds for the alcohol to fully dry before injecting — wet alcohol stings and can affect absorption
  • Wipe the top of your Tirzepatide vial with a fresh alcohol swab
6
Draw Up Your Dose
💉
▼
  • Remove the cap from your insulin syringe
  • Insert the needle into the Tirzepatide vial and draw back the plunger to your prescribed volume
  • Check for air bubbles — if present, gently tap the syringe and push them out
  • Double-check the volume before injecting
💡 Always draw from your reconstituted vial slowly. Air bubbles in a subcutaneous injection are not dangerous but can reduce your dose accuracy.
7
Inject
✦
▼
  • Gently pinch the skin at your chosen site with two fingers
  • Insert the needle at a 45–90° angle — 90° for abdomen/thigh, 45° for upper arm
  • Push the plunger down slowly and steadily
  • Hold the needle in place for 5–10 seconds after injecting — this ensures full delivery
  • Withdraw the needle at the same angle it entered
  • Apply gentle pressure with a gauze pad — do not rub
💡 A little redness or a small bump at the injection site is normal and will disappear within minutes. Mild stinging is also normal.
8
After Your Injection
🌿
▼
  • Dispose of your needle safely — recap carefully or use a sharps container
  • Never reuse syringes or needles
  • Store remaining vial in the refrigerator (2–8°C)
  • Eat a small meal within 30–60 minutes if possible
  • Stay hydrated throughout the day
  • Note the date, dose, and injection site in your weekly log
💡 Take your injection on the same day every week — set a phone alarm so you never miss a dose. Consistency is key to results! 📅

📊 What to Monitor Each Week

Every Week
Record your weight — same time, same conditions (morning, after using CR, before eating). Take note of any side effects experienced.
Every 4 Weeks
Check-in with MedGlow Wellness — send your weight, blood pressure, heart rate, and a photo update. Dose adjustment will be made here if needed.
Every 3–6 Months
Repeat laboratory tests — CBC with Platelet, FBS, SGPT/SGOT, Thyroid Panel, Serum Creatinine. Send results to MedGlow Wellness for review.
Anytime
Message MedGlow Wellness if you experience anything unusual — nausea, vomiting, severe abdominal pain, dizziness, or any concern at all. Do not hesitate. 📱

😌 Common Side Effects

These are normal, especially in the first few weeks. They usually improve as your body adjusts:

Nausea — especially after first injection
Decreased appetite
Constipation or loose stools
Mild fatigue
Mild injection site redness
Burping or bloating
✅ Tips to reduce nausea: Eat smaller meals · Avoid fatty/greasy food · Eat 3x a day — do not skip meals · Stay hydrated · Take injection at bedtime so you sleep through the nausea window

🚨 When to Seek Emergency Help

Go to the nearest emergency room immediately and message MedGlow Wellness if you experience:

  • 🔴 Severe abdominal pain (possible pancreatitis)
  • 🔴 Persistent vomiting — unable to keep food/water down
  • 🔴 Severe dizziness or fainting
  • 🔴 Signs of hypoglycemia — cold sweats, shaking, confusion, extreme hunger
  • 🔴 Difficulty breathing or swallowing
  • 🔴 Neck lump or difficulty swallowing (possible thyroid concern)
